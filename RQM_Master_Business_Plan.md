- Typical consultancy captures 5-15% of institution's external services budget
- RQM target: $350K Year 1 → $750K Year 2 → $1.5M Year 3

## Market Drivers

### 1. Federal Reproducibility Mandates (Immediate, Urgent)

**NIH Data Management & Sharing Policy (January 2023):**
- All NIH-funded research must include data management plans
- Data must be shared in accessible, reproducible formats
- Institutions face compliance deadlines; many are unprepared

**NSF Public Access Plan (2023):**
- Similar requirements for NSF-funded research
- Software and code must be preserved and shared
- Reproducibility requirements create immediate consulting demand

**Impact:** Research teams need help NOW to meet compliance requirements. This creates immediate, urgent demand for consultancies that can implement reproducible workflows, containerization, and data management solutions.

### 2. Reproducibility Crisis (Growing Awareness)

**The problem:** Studies estimate 50-70% of computational research cannot be reproduced due to:
- Undocumented software dependencies
- Lost or inaccessible code
- Insufficient computational environment documentation
- Poor data management practices

**Institutional response:**
- 89% of research institutions report inadequate computational support (2024 survey)
- Universities creating new RSE positions but cannot hire fast enough
- Growing recognition that reproducibility is not just ethical—it's required for publication and funding

**Market opportunity:** Institutions need external help to implement reproducibility best practices, train researchers, and audit existing workflows.

### 3. Research Software Engineer Shortage (Supply-Demand Imbalance)

**Demand side:**
- RSE roles growing 20%+ annually at universities
- Average time to fill RSE position: 6-9 months
- Many positions remain unfilled for 12+ months due to talent shortage

**Supply side:**
- Traditional CS graduates lack domain science training
- Domain scientists lack software engineering training
- Few graduate programs specifically training RSEs

**Why this matters for RQM:** Universities cannot build internal capacity fast enough. They need external consultancies to bridge the gap while they search for permanent hires. This creates sustained demand for 3-5+ years as the RSE ecosystem matures.

### 4. Data Volume Explosion (Infrastructure Strain)

**Growth rates by field:**
- Genomics: Data doubling every 12 months
- Materials science: High-throughput experiments generating TB-scale datasets
- Climate/environmental: Sensor networks creating continuous data streams
- Imaging (microscopy, astronomy): Petabyte-scale data management challenges

**Infrastructure challenge:**
- Most institutions lack expertise to manage, process, analyze these data volumes
- Cloud costs escalating; need optimization expertise
- On-premise HPC underutilized due to expertise gaps

**RQM opportunity:** Help institutions optimize infrastructure, build efficient pipelines, and train researchers to work at scale.

## Barriers to Entry

**Why new competitors will struggle to replicate RQM:**

### High Barriers

**1. Scientific credibility (PhD requirement)**
- Research community expects PhD-level expertise

- Can't be faked; PIs ask about dissertation, publications
- **Timeline to replicate:** 8-10 years

**2. Experimental + computational hybrid**
- Rare skill combination (most are one or the other)
- Requires wet lab training + computational training
- Most experimentalists who learn computation stay in academia
- **Timeline to replicate:** 10+ years (or find rare hire)

**3. Domain-specific networks**
- Takes years to build conference reputation
- Teaching roles provide access competitors can't get
- University connections develop over 5-10 years
- **Timeline to replicate:** 5-10 years

**4. Two complementary founders**
- Finding compatible co-founder with non-overlapping expertise is hard
- Swabir/Sanae combination is rare (physics + chemistry, both entrepreneurial)
- Most consultants are solo or founder + employee
- **Timeline to replicate:** Luck + years of searching

### Medium Barriers

**5. Track record and reputation**
- Need successful projects to get testimonials
- Case studies take 6-12 months to develop
- Reputation compounds over time
- **Timeline to replicate:** 2-3 years

**6. Service breadth**
- RQM offers 6 service lines (RSE, bioinfo, HPC, data science, training, retainer)

- Breadth requires diverse expertise
- **Timeline to replicate:** 3-5 years

### Low Barriers (Manageable)

**7. Technology/tools**

- Not a defensible moat alone
- **Timeline to replicate:** 6-12 months

**8. Business infrastructure**
- LLC formation, website, contracts are easy
- Can copy in weeks
- Not defensible
- **Timeline to replicate:** 1-3 months

**Bottom line:**

## Executive Summary

### Business Concept
IDRIS (Institute for Data-Driven Research & Innovation) is a specialized research computing and data science consultancy serving academic institutions, research centers, and health organizations. We enable reproducible, scalable scientific discovery through research software engineering, data pipeline development, and advanced computing infrastructure design.

### Market Opportunity
The research computing services market is experiencing significant growth driven by:
- Expanding NIH/NSF mandates for data management and reproducibility
- Increasing complexity of research data and computational requirements
- Shortage of research software engineers in academic settings
- Growing adoption of HPC, cloud computing, and containerized workflows in research

**Market Size:** The global scientific research software market is valued at $2.8B+ annually, with the academic segment growing at 12-15% CAGR.

### Competitive Advantage
- **Niche Specialization:** Focus on academic/federally funded research environments (vs. generic consulting)
- **Reproducibility Focus:** Alignment with NIH/NSF open science mandates
- **Flexible Engagement:** Project-based, subaward, and retainer models
- **Academic Fluency:** Understanding of grant cycles, institutional procurement, IRB requirements

### Financial Highlights (Year 1 Projections)
- **Revenue Target:** $150,000 - $250,000
- **Gross Margin:** 65-75%
- **Break-even:** Month 8-10
- **Initial Investment:** $15,000 - $25,000

### Success Metrics
- 8-12 client engagements in Year 1
- 2-3 recurring institutional partnerships by Year 2
- 80%+ client retention rate
- Expansion to 2 FTE by Year 3

## Feasibility Analysis

### 1. Market Feasibility ✅ STRONG

#### Demand Indicators
**Positive Factors:**
- NIH Data Management & Sharing Policy (effective Jan 2023) creates compliance needs
- NSF Public Access Plan 2.0 emphasizes reproducibility
- 89% of research institutions report inadequate research computing support (NSF survey)
- Growing "reproducibility crisis" drives demand for better practices
- Core facilities increasingly outsource specialized computing needs

**Target Market Size:**
- **Primary:** 200+ R1 universities in US
- **Secondary:** 150+ medical schools and research hospitals
- **Tertiary:** 50+ federal research labs and nonprofits
- **Estimated addressable market:** 5,000+ potential research groups/centers

**Market Validation:**
- Existing competitors (below) are thriving
- Research software engineering roles growing 20%+ annually
- Increasing grant budgets allocated to computational infrastructure

#### Competition Analysis

**Direct Competitors:**
1. **Ronin** - Cloud research platforms ($150-500/user/month)
   - Strength: Established platform
   - Weakness: Limited custom development, high ongoing costs

2. **2i2c** - Managed JupyterHub for research ($8-15k/month)
   - Strength: Open source focus
   - Weakness: Infrastructure-only, expensive for small teams

3. **Research Computing Consultancies** (e.g., Cambridge Consultants, Kitware)
   - Strength: Established reputation
   - Weakness: High rates ($200-400/hr), corporate focus

**Indirect Competitors:**
- University IT departments (limited bandwidth/expertise)
- Individual freelancers (inconsistent quality)
- Postdocs/students (high turnover, not sustainable)

**IDRIS Differentiation:**
- Lower price point than enterprise consultancies ($100-175/hr vs $200-400/hr)
- More specialized than freelancers
- More flexible than platforms
- Better aligned with academic culture than corporate consultancies

### Risk Analysis

#### Key Risks & Mitigation Strategies

**1. Sales Cycle Dependency**
- **Risk:** Long academic sales cycles create revenue gaps
- **Impact:** Cash flow challenges, unpredictable income
- **Probability:** Medium-High
- **Mitigation:**
  - Build 6-month operating reserve
  - Maintain diverse pipeline (12+ prospects)
  - Mix of project and retainer revenue
  - Part-time consulting backup initially

**2. Client Concentration**
- **Risk:** Over-dependence on 1-2 major clients
- **Impact:** Revenue loss if client leaves
- **Probability:** Medium (especially Year 1)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Target max 30% revenue from single client
  - Focus on retainer diversification
  - Strong client satisfaction to enable retention
  - Continuous business development

**3. Competition from Internal Resources**
- **Risk:** Universities build internal research software teams
- **Impact:** Reduced market opportunity
- **Probability:** Medium-Low (trend is happening but slowly)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Partner with university IT (not compete)
  - Focus on specialized/surge capacity needs
  - Target institutions without internal capacity
  - Emphasize flexibility and specialized expertise

**4. Pricing Pressure**
- **Risk:** Clients compare to student/postdoc labor rates
- **Impact:** Margin compression
- **Probability:** Medium
- **Mitigation:**
  - Emphasize total value (speed, quality, sustainability)
  - Clear ROI demonstration
  - Case studies showing long-term cost savings
  - Value-based pricing education

**5. Regulatory Changes**
- **Risk:** Changes to NIH/NSF policies affecting demand
- **Impact:** Could increase or decrease demand unpredictably
- **Probability:** Low-Medium
- **Mitigation:**
  - Monitor policy developments
  - Diversify service offerings
  - Position as policy compliance partner
  - Flexible service adaptation

**6. Technical Obsolescence**
- **Risk:** Falling behind on emerging technologies
- **Impact:** Loss of competitive edge
- **Probability:** Low (with proper investment)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Budget 5% time for learning/experimentation
  - Active in professional communities
  - Conference attendance
  - Continuous skill development

**7. Founder Capacity**
- **Risk:** Single point of failure, burnout
- **Impact:** Business disruption
- **Probability:** Medium
- **Mitigation:**
  - Clear work-life boundaries
  - Subcontractor network for backup
  - Documentation of all processes
  - Early hiring when revenue supports it

**Overall Risk Assessment:** MODERATE - Manageable with proper planning
